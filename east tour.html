East Bali Tour

Explore the cultural and natural wonders of East Bali. From the iconic Pura Lempuyang to the serene Taman Ujung, this tour offers a journey into the heart of Bali's traditional villages, ancient temples, and breathtaking landscapes. Experience the tranquility of Amed Beach and the historical charm of Tenganan Village.

Pura Lempuyang

Pura Lempuyang, often referred to as the "Gate of Heaven," is one of Bali's oldest and most revered temples. Situated at the summit of Mount Lempuyang, this sacred site offers unparalleled views of Mount Agung, making it a favorite destination for travelers and photographers. The journey to the top requires climbing over 1,700 steps, passing through lush forests and smaller temples along the way, adding to the spiritual experience. The iconic split gate, perfectly framing Mount Agung, is a must-visit for its symbolic and breathtaking beauty.

Taman Ujung

Taman Ujung, also known as the "Water Palace," is a historical site built during the Karangasem Kingdom. It features a harmonious blend of Balinese and European architectural styles, surrounded by tranquil gardens, large ponds, and scenic walkways. The main pavilion, perched on a hill, offers panoramic views of the surrounding rice fields, ocean, and Mount Agung in the distance. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or a stunning photo spot, Taman Ujung provides a serene escape steeped in history and beauty.

Besakih Temple

Known as the "Mother Temple" of Bali, Pura Besakih is the largest and holiest temple complex on the island. Located on the southwestern slopes of Mount Agung, the temple is comprised of over 80 smaller temples, each dedicated to various Hindu deities and natural elements. Its magnificent architecture and spiritual atmosphere make it a must-visit destination. Visitors are welcomed to explore the sprawling complex, learning about its deep cultural and religious significance, while enjoying the breathtaking views of Bali's lush landscapes.

Amed Beach

Amed is a peaceful coastal village in East Bali, known for its unique black sand beaches and vibrant coral reefs. It is a haven for divers and snorkelers, offering some of the best underwater experiences in Bali. Beyond its marine attractions, Amed provides a glimpse into the traditional Balinese way of life, with salt farming and fishing as key aspects of the local economy. Visitors can also enjoy stunning sunrise views over the ocean, making Amed a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure.

Tenganan Village

Desa Tenganan is one of Bali's most famous traditional villages, known for its unique culture and ancient rituals. Home to the Bali Aga people, the village has preserved its distinct customs, architecture, and weaving traditions for centuries. Visitors can explore the beautiful surroundings, witness the creation of intricate ikat textiles, and participate in traditional ceremonies. Desa Tenganan offers a fascinating look into Bali's past and a deeper understanding of its cultural heritage.
