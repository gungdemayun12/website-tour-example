Kintamani Tour

Kintamani is one of Bali's most famous destinations, known for its breathtaking views of Mount Batur, Lake Batur, and serene surroundings. Visitors can enjoy outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and moments of relaxation in this volcanic region. Here are the highlights of what you can experience in Kintamani

Mount Batur

Mount Batur is an active volcano and one of Bali's most iconic landmarks. Hiking to the summit for sunrise is a must-do experience, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and lake. The trek is suitable for beginners, and guides are available to ensure a safe and enjoyable journey. The volcanic landscape, combined with the early morning mist, creates an unforgettable adventure.

Batur Lake

Lake Batur is Bali's largest crater lake, located at the base of Mount Batur. Its tranquil waters are perfect for a relaxing boat ride or simply enjoying the serene atmosphere. The surrounding areas are dotted with small villages and lush greenery, offering an authentic glimpse into rural Balinese life. Visitors can also enjoy fresh seafood at the lakeside restaurants.

Trunyan Village

Trunyan Village is known for its unique funeral rituals, where bodies are placed under a sacred banyan tree instead of being buried or cremated. The village offers a fascinating insight into Bali's ancient traditions and cultural diversity. Visitors can reach the village by boat and learn about the sacred customs that have been preserved for centuries.

Toya Devasya Hot Spring

Toya Devasya is a luxurious hot spring resort located on the edge of Lake Batur. Guests can soak in the natural thermal pools while enjoying stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains. The resort also offers spa treatments, dining, and other facilities for a day of relaxation and rejuvenation.

Kintamani Coffee Plantation

Kintamani is famous for its coffee plantations, where visitors can learn about the traditional methods of growing and processing Bali's renowned coffee. The region's volcanic soil and high altitude make it ideal for producing high-quality coffee beans. A visit to a coffee plantation includes sampling various blends, including the famous Kopi Luwak, and enjoying scenic views of the lush farmland.
