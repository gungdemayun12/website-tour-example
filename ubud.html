Ubud Tour

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, offering a mix of nature, art, and history. From breathtaking rice terraces to sacred forests, Ubud is a must-visit destination for those seeking to immerse themselves in Balinese culture. Here's what you can experience in Ubud:

Monkey Forest

The Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary is a haven for hundreds of playful long-tailed macaques. This lush jungle area is not only a sanctuary for the monkeys but also houses ancient temples and statues. Visitors can stroll through the forest while observing the monkeys in their natural habitat. Respectful interaction is encouraged to maintain harmony between visitors and wildlife.

Campuhan Ridge Walk

Taman Ujung, also known as the "Water Palace," is a historical site built during the Karangasem Kingdom. It features a harmonious blend of Balinese and European architectural styles, surrounded by tranquil gardens, large ponds, and scenic walkways. The main pavilion, perched on a hill, offers panoramic views of the surrounding rice fields, ocean, and Mount Agung in the distance. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or a stunning photo spot, Taman Ujung provides a serene escape steeped in history and beauty.

Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace, also known as Puri Saren Agung, is a historical building complex located in the center of Ubud. The palace is home to intricate Balinese architecture and serves as a venue for traditional dance performances. Visitors can explore the well-maintained gardens and enjoy the cultural shows held here every evening.

Ubud Art Market

Ubud Art Market is the perfect place to shop for unique Balinese crafts and souvenirs. From handmade bags to colorful sarongs and intricate carvings, the market offers a wide array of locally crafted items. Bargaining is a common practice, making shopping here a fun and interactive experience.

Museum Puri Lukisan

Museum Puri Lukisan is the oldest art museum in Bali, showcasing traditional and modern Balinese art. Located in the heart of Ubud, it features collections of paintings and wood carvings that span centuries of Balinese history. Visitors can admire the intricate artistry and learn about the cultural heritage of Bali. The serene garden and tranquil setting make it a perfect spot for art enthusiasts and history lovers alike.
